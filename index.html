Understanding Decoloniality

Key principles and definitions from the reading

Core Principles

Based on "Decolonising Education in Africa: What does it mean" by Ndlovu-Gatsheni (2022), these are the key principles of decoloniality:

Key Principles of Decoloniality:

  • Decoloniality is not reverse colonialism or revenge against former colonizers
  • It seeks to rehumanise both those who were dehumanised and those who participated in dehumanisation
  • It involves pluriversalising knowledge - recognising all humans as knowledge producers
  • It requires deracialising power structures that perpetuate colonial hierarchies
  • It focuses on justice rather than vengeance, aiming to build humane societies

Three Forms of Coloniality:

  • Coloniality of Being: Doubting the humanity of colonised peoples and consigning them to subhuman conditions
  • Coloniality of Knowledge: Universalising Western knowledge while peripheralising Global South knowledge systems
  • Coloniality of Power: Creating racial categories and hierarchising humanity based on skin colour
  • These forms interconnect to create systemic injustice that persists beyond formal colonialism
  • Decolonisation requires dismantling these structures in education and society
